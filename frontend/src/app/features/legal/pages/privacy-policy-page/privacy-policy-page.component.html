Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how FSD App (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use our web application and related services (collectively, the “Service”). The Service is designed to help teams work with Airtable data through authorized integrations, including OAuth-based access to the Airtable API, optional synchronization into our databases, and in-product tools such as grids and filters for your connected content.

By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the Service.

1. Information we collect

Depending on how you use the Service, we may process the following categories of information:

  • Account and profile information. Identifiers and contact details you provide when you register or sign in, such as name, email address, and authentication credentials managed by us or an identity provider you choose.
  • Airtable connection data. When you connect Airtable, we receive and store OAuth tokens and related authorization metadata needed to call the Airtable API on your behalf, including access to bases (“projects”), tables, records (for example tickets or pages in your bases), and user directory information where your permissions allow it.
  • Synced Airtable content. Data retrieved from Airtable via the API (including field values and pagination results) may be stored in our systems—for example, in application databases—to power search, filtering, revision-related workflows, and display in the product UI.
  • Revision history and activity data. Where you enable features that rely on Airtable revision or activity endpoints, we may process history or changelog information (such as assignee or status changes) derived from Airtable. Some of this processing may involve handling responses that originate from Airtable’s services in formats such as HTML before we normalize and store structured results.
  • Session and security data. For certain integration flows, we may process browser session information associated with your Airtable account when required to complete a feature you initiate. We may also process one-time authentication factors (for example, an MFA code) that you submit through the Service solely to complete sign-in or re-authentication with Airtable; such values are handled transiently and are not used for unrelated purposes.
  • Technical and usage data. Standard server and application logs, device and browser type, IP address, timestamps, and diagnostic information used to operate, secure, and improve the Service.

2. How we use information

We use the information above to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service, including OAuth connections, data synchronization, and in-app data grids and search.
  • Authenticate you, enforce access controls, detect abuse, and protect the security and integrity of the Service.
  • Store and display Airtable-backed content you choose to sync or view, consistent with your permissions in Airtable.
  • Communicate with you about the Service, support requests, and important notices.
  • Comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests.

3. Legal bases (where applicable)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or similar jurisdictions, we rely on appropriate legal bases such as performance of a contract, legitimate interests (for example securing and improving the Service, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights), and consent where required for specific processing (such as certain cookies or marketing, if offered).

4. Sharing and subprocessors

We share information with service providers who assist us in hosting, storage, analytics, security, and communications. Airtable receives and processes information in accordance with your use of their platform and their own policies; see Airtable’s Privacy Policy for details on their practices.

We may disclose information if required by law, to protect rights and safety, or in connection with a business transaction (such as a merger or asset sale) subject to appropriate safeguards.

5. Retention

We retain information for as long as your account is active, as needed to provide the Service, and as necessary to comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. OAuth tokens and synced Airtable data may be deleted or refreshed when you disconnect an integration, revoke access in Airtable, or request deletion, subject to backup and legal retention requirements.

6. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and organizational measures designed to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, or alteration. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure; we encourage you to use strong passwords, protect MFA devices, and review connected integrations regularly.

7. Your rights and choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or export personal information, or to object to or restrict certain processing. You may also revoke Airtable’s authorization from your Airtable account settings, which limits further API access from our Service. To exercise rights with us, contact us using the channels we provide in the application or on our website.

8. International transfers

We may process information in countries other than where you live. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses or equivalent mechanisms.

9. Children

The Service is not directed to children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Changes

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will post the revised version and, where appropriate, provide additional notice.

11. Contact

For privacy questions or requests, please contact us through the contact method indicated in the Service or on our official site. If you use Airtable, you remain responsible for compliance with your organization’s policies and Airtable’s terms when connecting your bases to FSD App.
